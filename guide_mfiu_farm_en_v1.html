MIW — Managed Independent Work

2 MIW lectures (nos. 17 and 18) — studied independently, no scheduled date
Written notes must be prepared and presented to your teacher
MIW notes are required for admission to exam
Grading Criteria
10-point scale
10
Systematised, deep and complete knowledge across all sections; precise terminology; impeccable practical skills; creative solution of non-standard tasks; active creative work. confirmed at the exam by head of department
9
Deep and complete knowledge; creative solution of complex tasks; active independent work. No more than one minor inaccuracy per question.
8
Deep and complete knowledge; independent solution of complex tasks. No more than two minor errors per question.
7
Good systematised knowledge; independent solution of standard tasks. No more than three minor errors or one major error per question.
6
Sufficiently full and systematised knowledge; ability to solve typical tasks. No more than two major errors.
5
Sufficient knowledge; solves typical tasks with a little help. Errors including major ones are possible.
4
Sufficient knowledge; logical presentation without major errors; works under teacher's guidance. Pass.
3
Incomplete knowledge; significant logical errors; weak mastery of practical skills.
2
Fragmentary knowledge; gross logical errors; cannot solve standard tasks.
1
No knowledge; refusal to answer; absent without valid reason; disciplinary violation.

Credit

Admission to Credit

Positive current certification — all concluding classes / colloquiums passed
No unworked-off absences from classes and lectures
Notes of MIW lectures (managed independent work) presented
Protocols of practical classes completed and signed
No violations of discipline and safety rules

Credit Procedure

Passed current certification on the first attempt — "passed" mark based on current assessment results.
Passed on the second attempt or later — oral or written interview on all semester sections.
Second retake — before the commission in accordance with the Certification Rules.

⚠️ Pharmacy groups do not have a credit in this semester — admission to exam is based on completing all 17 classes and meeting the requirements above.

Exam

Admission to Exam

Positive current certification (concluding classes) for both semesters
No unworked-off absences from classes and lectures
MIW lecture notes submitted
Protocols of practical classes completed and signed
No violations of discipline and safety rules

Exam Format · Pharmacy (English groups)

Stage 1Computer test — 60 questions, 40 minutes. Students who achieve a passing score (60% and more) are admitted to Stage 2. Students who do not achieve 60% receive 1 (one) point in the record.
Stage 2Oral interview using examination tickets approved annually by the department. Ticket: 2 questions on mechanisms of regulation of physiological functions + 1 question on methods of investigation with a task.
RefusalRefusal to give an oral answer → examination mark = 1 (one) point.
PresentCompleted practical workbook + MIW lecture notes.
Final Examination Mark

The final examination mark (Mexam) is calculated taking into account the student rating for two semesters (Rstudy) and the mark for Stage 2, rounded to the nearest integer:

Mexam = (M2nd stage + Rstudy) / 2  +  B − P

where B — bonus points, P — penalty points.

Rstudy = (Racadem + Rpract + Rcurrent) / 3

Racadem — average mark for theoretical material in concluding classes (1st mark) for two semesters
Rpract — average mark for practical skills in concluding classes (2nd mark) for two semesters
Rcurrent — average grade for two semesters from the electronic journal
